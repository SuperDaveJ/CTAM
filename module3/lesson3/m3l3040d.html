Critical Thinking Traits

This animation shows selectable box-shaped tabs that when clicked, open in a box and the rest of the screen is watermarked. The following text appears when each of the tabs is clicked:

  • Intellectual Humility-means knowing what your knowledge limits are. 

  • Intellectual Courage-means having the need to face and fairly address ideas, beliefs or viewpoints when you have strong negative emotions about them and have not seriously considered the other point of view.

  • Intellectual Empathy-means having the need and willingness to put yourself in another’s shoes; to understand their point of view. 

  • Intellectual Autonomy-means having control of your own beliefs, values and inferences.   

  • Intellectual Integrity-means being true to your own thinking and consistent in the application of your own standards of thinking. 

  • Intellectual Perseverance-means having the need to use insights and truth in spite of obstacles. 

  • Confidence in Reason-knowing that with encouragement, people can think for themselves, come to their own conclusions, and form rational viewpoints despite deep seated obstacles in their own minds and among others.

  • Fairmindedness-requires you to treat all viewpoints respectfully and put your own feelings aside.
