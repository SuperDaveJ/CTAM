Forming Your Own Mindset

As a result of that group association, you formed a mindset about what anyone outside your own group thinks, says or does. Your belief may not be spot on, but it colored the way you thought of the other kids. That mindset extends throughout adulthood as well, although it may manifest itself differently.

An example of this is:

After 9/11, there were a rash of hate crimes against people of Middle Eastern descent, people wearing traditional Muslim garb and even Sikhs, an Indian subgroup that isn’t even Muslim or Middle Eastern. The hate crimes reflected a rapidly created mindset that associated with 9/11 crimes with the Muslim world.
