Mindsets and Their Importance

Mindsets are cognitive frameworks that help you make sense of new information, but can also color the way you understand that information.

It is a set of assumptions and/or biases that act as a lens (or filter) for understanding information. They shape the way we see the world; everything is seen through the lens of our particular mindsets.

In his book, The Thinker’s Toolkit, Morgan Jones states:

"Mindsets provide us instant insight into complex problems, enabling us to make timely, coherent, and accurate judgments about events. Without mindsets, the complexity and ambiguity of these problems would be unimaginable, if not paralyzing. Think of mindsets as information processing shortcuts."-Morgan Jones, The Thinker’s Toolkit, 1998
