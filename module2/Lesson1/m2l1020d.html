What is Critical Thinking?

A book with the title “Critical Thinking: An Introduction” is displayed on the screen. After the book is opened, the following text displays on the left page:

“Active, persistent, and careful consideration of any belief or supposed form of knowledge in the light of the grounds which support it and the further conclusion to which it tends.

- Alex Fisher, Critical Thinking: An Introduction, Cambridge Press, 2001.”

Definitions of the following words or phrases are displayed on the right page:

“Active”: This is in contrast to a passive process, in which you just receive ideas and information from someone else. The active process involves thinking things through, raising questions, and finding relevant information.

“Persistent and careful”: This is in contrast to unreflective thinking, in which you jump to a conclusion or make a snap judgment.

“Grounds which support further conclusions to which it tends”: What matters are the reasons we have for believing something and the implications of our beliefs!
