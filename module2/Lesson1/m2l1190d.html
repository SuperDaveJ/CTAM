Common Steps for Critical Thinking

Step 1: Identify the Problem
  1. Assemble specific known facts
  2. List what we think we know
  3. Identify what is not known
In every investigation, a set of facts is known at the onset; whether they are eye-witness accounts, crime scene data, or crime report information. Based on that data, you may make assumptions about what happened or did not happen. There are always a number of unknowns and that is what you are looking to find.

Step 2: Consider All Information Without Bias
  1. Do not rush to hypotheses or conclusions about the problem or incident
When you can look at all this data without bias, you can help prevent jumping to conclusions, and instead systematically look through the data to come up with every possible alternative that may have happened.
