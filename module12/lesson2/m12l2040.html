What’s Going On?

Let’s review how the brain takes in and understands information. Three main things to remember:

  • First, the information goes through the lens of “mindset.” Mindsets are frameworks that help make sense of new information, and are often formed through our associations with identity and organizational groups.

  • Second, information processing happens in two parts, perception and memory. Both impact the way your brain orders the information, and both pass through the lens of a mindset. In both cases, you process the information subjectively.

  • Finally, all of this takes place within the context of the particular situations of the thinker. In other words, whatever is going on in the life of the thinker also influences the perspective.
