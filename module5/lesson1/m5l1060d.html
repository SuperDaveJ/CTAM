Case Study

Let’s try this out. Suppose your supervisor asked you to consider this question:
“How much crime spillover are U.S. border communities experiencing as a result of Mexican drug cartels?”

Consider these questions:

  • Do you really understand what you are being asked for? If not, you should ask for more clarification.

  • What are you looking for?

  • What question are you trying to answer?

  • Is it the right question, or are there others you should consider?

  • What available resources should you use? Where should you look?

Based on your thinking what would your purpose be? What would your goal be?

Compare your purpose and goals to this expert response:

  • What type of crime is the focus of this question?

  • Is the crime violent or non-violent?

  • What locations on the border are to be considered?

  • Which Mexican drug cartels are to be considered?

  • Why is this information needed and what is it to be used for?
