Learner selects a photo to see an example of what the analyst is doing at each point.

Questions: Analysts rarely produce analysis without specific requirements, based on intelligence questions their decision makers want answered. These questions determine the direction of the analyst’s thinking.

Generating Hypotheses: The analyst reviews the intelligence data and begins to form answers to the intelligence questions. These answers are his hypotheses.

Testing Hypotheses: The analyst tests his hypotheses by reviewing more intelligence data and talking with other analysts. If the intellectual standards of his hypotheses hold up, then he can begin to believe that he’s on the right track.

Defending Judgments: Once the analyst has drawn his conclusions, he must be prepared to defend them. For example, the analyst may brief the conclusions to others and answer their probing questions.
