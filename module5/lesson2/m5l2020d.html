The Importance of Questions

Questions are an important tool to ensure that you get focused and accurate information and that you come to the right conclusions. Take a minute to see what some expert critical thinkers have to say about the importance of questions.

From The Role of Questions in Teaching, Thinking, and Learning:

  • Thinking is not driven by answers but by questions.

  • Questions define tasks, express problems and delineate issues. Answers often signal a full stop in thought. Only when an answer generates a further question does thought continue its life as such.

From Critical Thinking and Intelligence Analysis by David T. Moore:

  • Questions provide a formal means by which an analyst confronts biases about likely outcomes.

  • Questions serve to alert analysts to the notion that they might be deceived.

Drs. Paul and Elder state that questions guide your thinking, and if the question is vague, your thinking will lack clarity.
