Brainstorming Phases

This graphic shows the two phases of brainstorming: Divergent and Convergent. Divergent is shown as a triangle starting from a question or problem and expanding out, with the following explanatory text: Divergent - involves thinking to generate and collect new ideas and insights. Convergent is shown as a triangle that narrows down to an answer or solution, with the following explanatory text: Convergent - involves grouping those ideas and organizing them around key concepts.

When the Divergent and Convergent phases of the animation are selected, the following text is displayed:

  • Divergent: Involves thinking to generate and collect new ideas and insights

  • Convergent: Involves grouping those ideas and organizing them around key concepts
