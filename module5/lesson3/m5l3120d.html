Narrow Hypotheses to a Reasonable Number

The graphic initially shows image of Convergent triangle of Brainstorming Analytical Method overlaid with text: “Narrow Hypotheses to a Reasonable Number.” When it is selected, the following text is overlaid on top of the graphic:
“There is no ‘correct’ number of hypotheses to be considered. The number depends upon the nature of the analytical problem and how advanced you are in the analysis of it. As a general rule, the greater your level of uncertainty, or the greater the policy impact of your conclusion, the more alternatives you may wish to consider. More than seven hypotheses may be unmanageable; if there are this many alternatives, it may be advisable to group several of them together for your initial cut at the analysis.” – Richard Heuer.
