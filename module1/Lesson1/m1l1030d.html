Planning & Direction: The planning and direction step in the intelligence cycle is a process in which you define the requirement, determine what you already know about the requirement, and develop a plan to gather the necessary intelligence.

Collection: The collection step in the intelligence cycle is a process in which you collect information. This collection process could include reviewing resources such as magazine articles, newspapers, hidden camera or listening device results, etc.

Processing & Exploitation: In the processing and exploitation step, you compile, clean, and record the data for later analysis. Examples of this could be textual descriptions or summaries from a document or a photograph description.

Analysis & Production: The analysis and production step in the intelligence cycle is a process in which you integrate, evaluate, and analyze information in order to identify significant facts and derive conclusions in preparation for inclusion in a variety of intelligence and other reporting products.

Dissemination: In the dissemination step, you distribute the intelligence products to the policy and decision makers who requested the information.
