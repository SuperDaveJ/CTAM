Negative vs. Positive Behavior

Here’s a very simple example of how our view of internal and external causes of behavior can change, depending on whose behavior we’re judging and whether it’s positive or negative. Suppose you are a baseball player.

If you strike out during the baseball game, it’s because the sun was in your eyes.

If a player on the other team strikes out, it’s because he is not as good a baseball player.

If you hit a homerun during the baseball game, it’s because you are a great baseball player.

If a player on the other team hits a homerun, it’s because she got lucky.
