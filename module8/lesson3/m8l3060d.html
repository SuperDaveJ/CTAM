Here’s an example related to your work as an analyst:
Richards J. Heuer reports that during the 1978-1979 Egypt-Israel peace negotiations, each party attributed had very different perspectives on signing the treaty and on each other’s past behavior. Compare each country’s perspectives.

What Israel believed:

  • Egypt’s willingness to make peace resulted from a deteriorating economy and a growing awareness of Israel’s military superiority.

  • Israel’s own willingness to compromise was a sign of their ever-present preference for peace.

  • Egypt’s undesirable behaviors, such as their past tendency to issue threats of annihilation, were a result of Egypt’s inherent opposition to a Jewish state.

  • Egypt’s desirable behavior of ceasing threats emanated from external circumstances, such as Israel's relative military superiority.

What Egypt believed:

  • Israel’s willingness to make peace resulted from external pressures, such as positive inducements and threats of negative sanctions by U.S.

  • Egypt’s own willingness to sign a treaty was due to their inherent disposition for peace. However,

  • Israel’s establishment of settlements on the West Bank of the Jordan River was because of Zionist expansionism.

  • If Israel chose not to place settlements, that was because of external constraints, such as Western condemnation of settlements.
