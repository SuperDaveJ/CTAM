Internal vs. External Causes of Behavior

One of the cognitive errors made in relation to perceiving what is expected is an inequity in assigning internal or external causes of behavior. What does this mean?

Internal Causes of Behavior
Internal causes of behavior include a person's attitudes, beliefs, and personality.

In assessing other people’s behavior, we tend to:

  • Overestimate the role of internal factors for negative behaviors

  • Underestimate the role of internal factors for positive ones

External Causes of Behavior
External causes include incentives and constraints, role requirements, social pressures, or other forces over which the individual has little control.

In assessing other people’s behavior, we tend to:

  • Underestimate the role of external factors for negative behaviors

  • Overestimate the role of external factors for positive behaviors
