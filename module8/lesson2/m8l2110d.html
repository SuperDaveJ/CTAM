Identifying Good Supporting Evidence

Earlier, you learned about hypothesis generation. In the process of determining good evidence, you must identify evidence that either supports or refutes that hypothesis.

But what is evidence? Here are two types you may be familiar with.

Criminal Evidence
Criminal evidence is any matter of fact or thing which proves or tends to prove the guilt or innocence of one charged with a criminal offense.

Intelligence Analysis Evidence
Evidence used for intelligence analysis can be defined as:

Thing or things helpful in forming a conclusion or judgment.

For example, the broken window was evidence that a burglary had taken place.

Analysts weigh the evidence for and against a hypothesis.
