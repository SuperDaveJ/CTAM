Simplistic Cause and Effect Cognitive Errors—Example 1

Now, let’s look at some examples of cognitive errors in which two events always occur in the same sequence, giving the inaccurate perception of cause and effect.

For example, did you know that consumption of ice cream goes up at the same time that homicides increase? Does ice cream consumption cause people to kill each other?

Of course not. Ice cream consumption and the homicide rate are positively correlated by time only. Hot weather is the reason for the increase in ice cream consumption as well as the increase in homicides.
