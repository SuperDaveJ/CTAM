Memory’s Subjective Aspect

It turns out that memory is far more subjective than these mechanical metaphors might suggest. It’s important to understand the ways in which memory is subjective because this subjectivity impacts different perspectives.

Memory is highly complex and scientists continue to study it to better understand how it works. You will see three concepts in this lesson-Encoding, Storage and Retrieval. Like perception, each concept relates to how information is processed in our minds, and that processing can be skewed, creating new perspectives.
