All these suggest mechanical accuracy, as if the mind were some sort of meticulous transcriber of our experiences. As a recorder, the brain does a notoriously wretched job. Tragedies and humiliation seem to be etched most sharply, often with the most unbearable exactitude, while those memories we think we really need, the name of the acquaintance, the time of the appointment, and the location of the car keys, have a habit of evaporating.
