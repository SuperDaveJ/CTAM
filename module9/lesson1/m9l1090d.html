Memory and Objectivity

A famous psychology experiment is about a video of a car accident. After the video, watchers were separated into groups and asked the same question about the accident. Only one word was changed in the question. “How fast were the cars going when they…”

  • Smashed…41mph

  • Collided…39mph

  • Bumped…38mph

  • Hit………34mph

  • Contacted…32mph

One week later, the experimenters asked if there was broken glass by the scene of the accident. People from the “smashed” group reported seeing glass more often than other groups. Nineteen percent of all people reported seeing broken glass.

There was no broken glass anywhere in the video of the accident. The researchers were able to manipulate the way information was encoded in the peoples’ brains, just by changing one word. Their memories of the events were not objective.
