Step 6: Evaluate Hypotheses Down The Columns

Focus on disproving hypotheses rather than proving one. Tally the pieces of evidence that are inconsistent and consistent with each hypothesis to see which explanations are the weakest and strongest. People have a natural tendency to concentrate on confirming hypotheses they already believe to be true, and they commonly give more weight to information that supports a hypothesis than to information that weakens it. Step five turns that thinking around.

Work down the columns now, looking at each hypothesis as a whole. By looking at the evidence and diagnostic information in this matrix, the hypotheses can compete for your favor on equal ground.
