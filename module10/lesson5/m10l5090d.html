Step 2: Construct a matrix, and add hypotheses

Your hypotheses should be as mutually exclusive as possible, to ensure you can distinctly evaluate each one.

There are a number of ACH matrices available on the market and on the Internet. For our purposes, let’s look at a simplistic matrix.

When the matrix icon is selected, a partially populated ACH matrix is displayed. Notice the matrix requires the hypotheses to be written across the horizontal axis, and the supporting evidence on the vertical axis. Also note the total row at the bottom of the matrix. The total row is used for adding up the weighted values for each piece of evidence in the hypothesis. It shows an ACH Table of 4 columns titled Hypothesis 1 through 4 on top and 5 rows on left titled Evidence and Total. Hypothesis columns are highlighted. Hypotheses are added to the hypotheses columns: H1 vehicles are stolen for use in Mexico, H2 vehicles are stolen for joyriding by locals and H3 vehicles are stolen for transportation northbound.
