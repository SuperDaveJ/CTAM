Step 7: Rank Hypotheses By Weakness Of Inconsistent Evidence

Ordering the hypotheses by the number of "Inconsistencies" gives you a rough ranking of their probability (least number of inconsistencies is probably the most likely). This is probably the most non-intuitive step in the process. Our minds like to rank things based on consistency, not inconsistency—so this strikes many of us as a bit odd.

In "A Consoling Word About Consistent Evidence", Morgan Jones recognizes this tension, and reminds us that "inconsistent evidence plays the key role during the analytic phase while consistent evidence plays the key role in the summation and presentation phase."
