Step 3: List Significant Evidence

ACH demands that after you explicitly identify all reasonable alternative hypotheses, you then array the evidence against each hypothesis, rather than evaluating the plausibility of each hypothesis one at a time. Just how do you decide what is significant?

  • Think broadly; do not limit yourself to concrete evidence, but list all factors that impact your judgment.

  • Include your own assumptions or logical deductions about people, groups or country's intentions, goals, or standard procedures.

  • Include the absence of things you would expect to see if a hypothesis were true.
