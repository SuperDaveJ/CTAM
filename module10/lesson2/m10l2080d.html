When Do Analysts Use Creative Thinking?

As an analyst, you’ll use creative thinking to help you broaden your perspective on the data and to help avoid cognitive errors. Let’s see how these analysts are using creative thinking.

Generate Ideas
This analyst is at the beginning of his intelligence project, and is using creative thinking to generate hypotheses. Remember the Brainstorm Analytical method? He’s using that to generate as many ideas as possible.

Stuck
This analyst has been working on her project for a while and feels stuck. She doesn’t really have any clear findings and no new ideas to get her going again. Creative thinking could help her get unstuck.

Overconfident
This analyst has a lot of data and feels very confident in his findings. However, his conclusions seem too predictable. He knows that overconfidence is a cognitive error that could lead him to ignore potential alternatives and meaningful evidence. So,he’s going to do some creative thinking to test his current conclusions.

Closed to New Ideas
The people in this group are not listening to each other’s perspectives. Each person feels very uncomfortable with some of the ideas that others are proposing. Creative thinking can help them to listen and appreciate each other’s perspectives.
