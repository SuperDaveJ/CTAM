Connecting Different Ideas

The last strategy for stimulating creative thought that we’ll cover is connecting different ideas. Take a look at some ways that you can connect different ideas. Then we’ll try some examples.

Cross Pollination

Cross-pollination in creative thinking means carrying an existing idea from one area into a completely new area to form even better ideas.

The trick is to try to explore worlds that may, at first glance, seem to have little relevance to the problems at hand. Connecting these divergent ideas stimulates creating thinking.

Seek Alternative Methods

Most people surround themselves with others who agree with them, since it is more comfortable to talk with people who share the same basic mindset.

Instead seek out differing viewpoints:

  • Talk to people who disagree with your ideas and listen to their input.

  • Have your analytic reports read by non-group members who have not been immersed in the issue.

  • Look to other disciplines for inspiration on your issues.

Random Association

De Bono suggests finding a random word, picture or concept and thinking about how associations between the word and your issue.

Don’t spend too much time trying to come up with the “perfect” random word, as that will send you back to your old ideas. It’s easy to find words:

  • From the dictionary

  • By flipping through a magazine and finding a picture

  • Looking around the office and choosing a piece of equipment
